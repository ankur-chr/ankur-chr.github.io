Inside the Model — a series

№1 · The World Inside

It only read words. Then it drew a map.

A 155,000-parameter network is trained to do one thing: predict the next move-symbol. It is never shown a grid, a map, or a coordinate — yet to do its job it builds a model of the world and hides it in its activations. A linear probe reads that world out, live. Then you reach in and lie to it — and it acts on the false belief, even hallucinating things that aren't there.

Runs entirely in your browser. No server. Every claim backed by a measured test.

The idea & the audience

Why this matters

If a toy network builds and uses an internal world model, the AI systems you actually rely on do too — at a scale we can't fully see. That's the whole game in interpretability and AI governance: in high-stakes domains like finance, "the model said so" isn't enough — we need to know what it believes and why.

These demos are my way of arguing that case in public — not with opinions, but with things you can run. Read the full essay →
